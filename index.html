Environmental Impact

Animal agriculture is responsible for 14.5% of global greenhouse gas emissions - more than all transportation combined. By going vegan, you can reduce your carbon footprint by up to 73%.

Livestock farming uses 83% of farmland while providing only 18% of calories. A vegan diet requires only 1/3 of the land needed for meat-based diets.

🐄

Animal Cruelty

Over 70 billion land animals are slaughtered annually for food worldwide. In India alone, millions of animals suffer in factory farms despite cultural traditions of vegetarianism.

Modern animal agriculture prioritizes profit over welfare, with animals confined in spaces so small they can't turn around or stretch their limbs. 99% of farm animals in the US are raised in factory farms.

💚

Health Benefits

Well-planned vegan diets are appropriate for all stages of life and may reduce risk for heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. Vegans typically have lower BMI and cholesterol levels.

Plant-based diets are rich in fiber, antioxidants, and beneficial phytochemicals. Studies show vegans have 15% lower mortality rates compared to meat-eaters.
